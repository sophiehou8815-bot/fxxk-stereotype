Proposal for Tempio del Futuro Perduto — Milan

FXXK
STEREOTYPE

Against imposed roles. Toward self-naming.

A participatory exhibition and electronic music project
on identity, pressure, and the right to become.

[ Image — blurred high-contrast portraiture / body fragment ]

Opening Statement

We inherit names before we
choose language for ourselves.

Worker. Mother. Outsider. Good girl. Breadwinner. Foreigner.
Useful roles. Violent reductions. Social scripts repeated until they begin to feel natural.

But identity is never singular.
And no life can be contained by the labels that make it legible to others.

FXXK STEREOTYPE is a multidisciplinary project about that break:
the distance between imposed identity and lived selfhood,
between social recognition and personal authorship.

It asks not only how we are seen,
but how we reclaim the right to define ourselves together.

Why This Format

This project is designed as a shared experience.

Image, text, sound, body, and public contribution speak to the same question.

The exhibition gives form to social roles, stereotype, contradiction, and reinvention.
The music turns those tensions into collective rhythm.
Participation allows the audience to move from witness to contributor.

This is not simply a show.
It is a space for collective re-articulation.

Venue-Specific Rationale

For Tempio del
Futuro Perduto

I believe FXXK STEREOTYPE could resonate strongly with Tempio del Futuro Perduto because it is conceived not only as a cultural event, but as a community-facing, multidisciplinary format.

Its structure aligns with a space where electronic music, visual expression, experimentation, and participation already coexist. The project can open outward through an online open call, inviting artists and contributors to respond to the theme of imposed roles and self-definition, and then bring those voices into one shared physical environment.

For Tempio, I imagine the project as:

a participatory exhibition,

a social and visual archive of contemporary identity pressure,

and an electronic ritual of rupture, release, and redefinition.

Format

Open Call

Inviting artists, writers, image-makers, and the public to submit works or testimonies around stereotype, role pressure, and self-definition.

Exhibition

Visual, textual, audio, and mixed-media contributions gathered into a curatorial environment.

Electronic Music

A live or DJ-led sonic progression from discipline to fracture to liberation.

Participation

Audience text, voice, or live declaration integrated into the experience.

Closing

For Tempio, FXXK STEREOTYPE would not be just an event to host,
but a proposition to build with:

socially aware but not didactic,
emotionally charged but open,
rooted in nightlife, yet reaching beyond it.
A project about the moment a label stops holding.
And something more alive begins.
