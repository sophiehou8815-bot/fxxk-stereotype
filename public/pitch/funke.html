Proposal for Funke — Ghent

FXXK
STEREOTYPE

Break the label. Name yourself.

A hybrid exhibition and electronic music concept
on identity, projection, and self-definition.

[ Image — blurred high-contrast portraiture / silhouette ]

Opening Statement

We are all read
before we are known.

By family. By work. By gender. By culture.
By the roles we perform too well.

Daughter. Boss. Employee. Lover. Son. Immigrant. Provider.
Necessary identities, perhaps. But never the whole story.

FXXK STEREOTYPE begins there:
in the tension between who we are expected to be
and who we become when we reclaim the right to define ourselves.

This project brings together exhibition, electronic music, and audience participation under one shared curatorial thread.
Not as separate formats, but as one continuous experience.

Why This Format

This is not an exhibition
next to a party.

It is one concept unfolding through multiple forms.

Visual works create reflection, confrontation, and recognition.
Electronic music transforms the same tension into movement, repetition, pressure, rupture, and release.
The audience is not only invited to watch, but to locate themselves inside the question.

Who names us?

Which labels shape us?

Which ones protect us, and which ones confine us?

Venue-Specific Rationale

For Funke

I see FXXK STEREOTYPE as a natural fit for Funke because the project lives exactly in the intersection that makes the space distinctive: club culture, art, curiosity, atmosphere, and identity exploration.

Rather than proposing a standard event booking, I imagine this as a carefully curated concept night:

an exhibition-led arrival,

a gradual deepening of mood and tension,

and an electronic set that turns reflection into physical release.

The project would resonate with a space that already understands nightlife not only as entertainment, but as a site of presence, experimentation, and becoming.

Format

Exhibition

Visual, textual, photographic, sound-based, or moving-image works responding to imposed identities and chosen selves.

Electronic Music

A sonic arc moving through control, rupture, and release.

Participation

Optional audience intervention: "They told me I should be ____ / I choose to be ____."

Closing

For Funke, I imagine FXXK STEREOTYPE as an intimate but high-impact hybrid evening:
visually sharp, emotionally resonant, and physically alive.
A space to reflect.
A space to move.
A space to become unreadable again.
